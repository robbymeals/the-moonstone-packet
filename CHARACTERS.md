# CHARACTERS — The Moonstone Narrative Packet

## Major Characters (Full Treatment)

---

### Gabriel Betteredge

**Role/Position**: House steward to Lady Julia Verinder at the Yorkshire estate; has served the family for over fifty years. Primary narrator of the First Period.

**What They Know and When**:
- Knows the household intimately — servants' characters, family history, the layout of every room
- Learns of the Diamond's curse from Franklin Blake before the birthday dinner
- Witnesses the paint on the door the morning after the theft (the decorators' work was still wet)
- Discovers Rosanna's agitation and her visits to the Shivering Sand, but doesn't understand why
- Never learns the full truth until Sergeant Cuff's final report; believes in Franklin's innocence throughout
- Knows Rosanna had been a thief before reforming at the Reformatory

**What They Want**:
- To protect the family's reputation and Miss Rachel's happiness
- To maintain order in the household
- To vindicate Franklin Blake, whom he loves like a son
- Simple peace and the guidance of Robinson Crusoe

**What They Hide**:
- His deep superstitious nature (constantly consults Robinson Crusoe as an oracle)
- His grief over Rosanna's death, which he blames himself for not preventing
- His suspicion that something was deeply wrong with Rosanna before her suicide

**Key Actions**:
- Welcomes Franklin Blake and becomes his ally throughout
- Reports Rosanna's strange behavior to Sergeant Cuff
- Accompanies Sergeant Cuff during the investigation
- Later helps Franklin Blake discover Rosanna's hidden letter in the Shivering Sand
- Narrates the First Period with warmth, digression, and limited perspective

---

### Franklin Blake

**Role/Position**: Nephew of Lady Julia Verinder; cousin and later husband to Rachel. Educated abroad (Germany, France, Italy). Entrusted with transporting the Moonstone from London.

**What They Know and When**:
- Knows the Diamond's history and curse from Colonel Herncastle's will
- Believes he delivered the Diamond safely to Rachel on her birthday
- Has NO memory of taking the Diamond due to the laudanum's effects
- Learns from Rachel (much later) that she SAW him take the Diamond from her sitting room
- Discovers from Rosanna's posthumous letter that she hid his paint-stained nightgown
- Finally learns from Ezra Jennings's reconstruction that Dr. Candy drugged him
- Participates in the opium experiment that proves he took the Diamond while unconscious

**What They Want**:
- Rachel's love and trust
- To clear his name and understand what happened
- To solve the mystery of his own actions
- To restore the family's honor

**What They Hide**:
- His foreign-educated ways create friction (the multiple "sides" of his character from different countries)
- He was sleepwalking under opium influence and had no conscious knowledge of taking the Diamond
- The paint smear on his nightgown proved he touched the freshly painted door

**Key Actions**:
- Brings the Moonstone to Yorkshire
- Unknowingly takes the Diamond while drugged and carries it toward his bedroom
- Is observed by Godfrey Ablewhite, who takes it from his hand
- Returns a year later to investigate and clear his name
- Undergoes the opium experiment to prove his unconscious actions
- Marries Rachel after the truth emerges

---

### Rachel Verinder

**Role/Position**: Daughter of Lady Julia Verinder; heiress; recipient of the Moonstone on her eighteenth birthday. Later marries Franklin Blake.

**What They Know and When**:
- Knows she received the Diamond at the birthday dinner
- **SAW Franklin Blake enter her sitting room and take the Diamond** around 1 a.m. on the night of the theft
- Does NOT know about the laudanum or that Franklin was unconscious
- Believes Franklin stole the Diamond deliberately, possibly to pay debts
- Refuses to expose him because she loves him
- Learns the truth only after Ezra Jennings's experiment proves Franklin's innocence

**What They Want**:
- To protect Franklin Blake from exposure, even at cost to herself
- To understand why the man she loves would steal from her
- To maintain her principles — she will not betray him but cannot forgive perceived treachery

**What They Hide**:
- The identity of who she saw take the Diamond (she tells no one, not even her mother)
- Her continuing love for Franklin despite believing him a thief
- The agony of her position — protecting someone she believes guilty

**Key Actions**:
- Refuses to assist Sergeant Cuff's investigation
- Becomes hostile and withdrawn, mystifying everyone
- Rejects Franklin's advances after the theft
- Eventually reveals to Franklin what she saw, breaking the case open
- Accepts Franklin after the truth is revealed

---

### Rosanna Spearman

**Role/Position**: Second housemaid at the Verinder estate; former thief reformed at a Reformatory where Lady Julia found her and gave her a position.

**What They Know and When**:
- Recognizes Sergeant Cuff as the detective who once arrested her
- Discovers Franklin's paint-stained nightgown in the wash
- Understands immediately that the paint smear connects Franklin to the scene of the theft
- Falls desperately in love with Franklin Blake
- Decides to protect him by destroying the evidence — makes a new nightgown and hides the original
- Realizes Franklin doesn't love her and never will

**What They Want**:
- Franklin Blake's love (hopeless)
- To protect him from accusation, even if she can never have him
- To escape her past as a thief and be seen as worthy
- Death, ultimately, as the only escape from her impossible situation

**What They Hide**:
- Her love for Franklin (transparent to Betteredge and Cuff, invisible to Franklin)
- The existence and location of the hidden nightgown
- Her plan to conceal evidence
- The letter she leaves explaining everything, hidden in the Shivering Sand

**Key Actions**:
- Secretly purchases fabric in Frizinghall to make a replacement nightgown
- Hides the original stained nightgown in a tin case, buried in the Shivering Sand
- Creates the chain of clues (letter held by Limping Lucy, tin case location in the quicksand)
- Drowns herself in the Shivering Sand when she realizes Franklin doesn't care for her
- Her posthumous letter reveals the nightgown evidence to Franklin a year later

---

### Sergeant Cuff

**Role/Position**: Celebrated detective from London, called in by the Verinders' lawyer to investigate the theft. Famous for his rose-growing hobby.

**What They Know and When**:
- Reads the household dynamics expertly from the start
- Quickly suspects Rachel herself, based on her refusal to cooperate
- Recognizes Rosanna Spearman from a previous case
- Deduces Rosanna is hiding something connected to the paint smear
- Identifies the Shivering Sand as significant
- Makes a wrong deduction: believes Rachel and Rosanna conspired, with Rachel pawning the Diamond to pay debts
- Later, in his final report, reveals the complete truth about Godfrey Ablewhite

**What They Want**:
- To solve the case cleanly
- Professional reputation
- To retire and grow roses (his constant dream)

**What They Hide**:
- His certainty about Rachel's involvement (later proven partially wrong)
- His method of investigation — appears casual while observing everything
- Professional detachment masking genuine interest in the human drama

**Key Actions**:
- Investigates the theft, questions servants, follows Rosanna's trail
- Deduces the paint smear is evidence and searches for the stained garment
- Correctly identifies Rosanna's involvement but misunderstands its nature
- Forced to withdraw when Lady Julia fires him to protect Rachel
- Returns at novel's end to deliver the final report identifying Godfrey as the thief

---

### Godfrey Ablewhite

**Role/Position**: Cousin to Rachel Verinder; celebrated philanthropist and "ladies' committee man"; secret embezzler.

**What They Know and When**:
- Knows the Diamond is valuable and desirable
- Witnessed Franklin Blake take the Diamond while sleepwalking
- Saw an opportunity when Franklin, drugged, dropped the Diamond
- Knows his own financial situation: he has embezzled funds from a trust he manages for a young ward
- Knows the Indians are pursuing the Diamond
- Knows he must redeem the Diamond from Luker before his trust fraud is discovered

**What They Want**:
- Money to cover his embezzlement before discovery
- To maintain his reputation as a virtuous philanthropist
- To marry Rachel (for her money) — proposes immediately after the theft
- To escape consequences

**What They Hide**:
- His life as an embezzler with a secret second establishment (mistress and expenses)
- That he took the Diamond from Franklin's unconscious hand
- That he pledged the Diamond to Luker the money-lender
- His complete moral bankruptcy beneath the charitable facade

**Key Actions**:
- Proposes to Rachel the day after the theft (she refuses)
- Takes the Diamond from the drugged Franklin and keeps it secret
- Pledges it to Luker for a loan, planning to redeem it when his ward's trust comes due
- Returns to redeem the Diamond from Luker a year later
- Is tracked, caught, and murdered by the three Indians, who recover the Moonstone
- His body is found with the Diamond missing; his double life is exposed posthumously

---

### Ezra Jennings

**Role/Position**: Medical assistant to Dr. Candy; a mixed-race man with a ruined reputation from some unspecified past scandal. Dying of an incurable illness, manages his pain with opium.

**What They Know and When**:
- Knows the effects of opium intimately from his own use
- Discovers Dr. Candy's delirium after his fever reveals suppressed guilt
- Painstakingly reconstructs Dr. Candy's fragmentary ravings into coherent meaning
- Realizes Dr. Candy gave Franklin laudanum as a "joke" to disprove Franklin's skepticism about medicine
- Understands this explains Franklin's sleepwalking and memory loss
- Devises the experiment to prove Franklin acted unconsciously

**What They Want**:
- To help Franklin Blake (a kindred outcast) clear his name
- To prove his medical theories about opium and memory
- To die with some meaning and vindication
- Human connection in his isolated existence

**What They Hide**:
- The nature of his past disgrace (never revealed; he insists he is innocent)
- His imminent death from his illness
- The depth of his loneliness and suffering
- His personal use of opium, which gives him insight into the case

**Key Actions**:
- Befriends Franklin Blake despite their social distance
- Reconstructs Dr. Candy's fever-speech to discover the laudanum dosing
- Designs and oversees the opium experiment replicating conditions of the theft night
- The experiment succeeds: Franklin reenacts taking the Diamond while unconscious
- Dies shortly after, having achieved his purpose; his journal is left to Franklin

---

### Miss Clack

**Role/Position**: Poor cousin to Lady Julia Verinder; evangelical Christian; distributor of religious tracts. Narrator of the Second Period (First Narrative).

**What They Know and When**:
- Knows Godfrey Ablewhite is active in Ladies' Committees and charities
- Present in London after Lady Julia's death
- Witnesses Godfrey's proposal to Rachel and her refusal
- Learns of the reading of Lady Julia's will
- Does NOT know Godfrey's true character
- Knows nothing of the investigation's actual progress

**What They Want**:
- To save souls through religious conversion
- To benefit from Lady Julia's will (receives nothing)
- To position herself close to Rachel and the family's wealth
- Recognition for her piety

**What They Hide**:
- Her mercenary interest beneath evangelical concern
- Her jealousy of Rachel
- Her infatuation with Godfrey Ablewhite
- Her satisfaction at others' misfortunes (poorly hidden)

**Key Actions**:
- Narrates the London period with heavy religious bias
- Plants religious tracts everywhere
- Documents Godfrey's public saintliness (ironically, since he's secretly corrupt)
- Provides a comic/satirical perspective that Collins uses to critique Victorian piety

---

### Matthew Bruff

**Role/Position**: Family lawyer to the Verinders; practical, trustworthy, secular counterweight to Miss Clack.

**What They Know and When**:
- Knows the family's legal and financial situation
- Knows the terms of Lady Julia's will and Colonel Herncastle's bequest
- Suspects something is wrong with Rachel but doesn't know what
- Gradually pieces together elements of the mystery from his legal position
- Learns Godfrey is managing a trust that may be compromised
- Confirms Godfrey's embezzlement after investigating

**What They Want**:
- To protect the Verinder family interests
- To serve justice through proper channels
- To understand the mystery without scandal

**What They Hide**:
- His professional discretion conceals his suspicions
- His growing alarm about Godfrey Ablewhite
- His knowledge of trust fund irregularities

**Key Actions**:
- Narrates part of the Second Period
- Investigates Godfrey's financial dealings
- Helps coordinate the final investigation
- Serves as the voice of legal propriety throughout

## Minor Characters (Brief Treatment)

---

### Penelope Betteredge

**Role/Position**: Daughter of Gabriel Betteredge; lady's maid to Rachel Verinder.

**What They Know**: Close to Rachel; observes her mistress's moods; knows Rosanna was acting strangely and visiting the Shivering Sand.

**What They Want**: Her mistress's happiness; to serve faithfully.

**What They Hide**: Nothing significant; she's observant but innocent.

**Key Actions**: Reports to her father about Rosanna's behavior and Rachel's distress; provides intelligence to Betteredge throughout.

---

### Lady Julia Verinder

**Role/Position**: Widow; mistress of the Yorkshire estate; mother to Rachel; sister to the late Colonel Herncastle.

**What They Know**: Knows the Diamond's curse and Colonel Herncastle's disreputable history; senses Rachel is hiding something but cannot penetrate it.

**What They Want**: To protect Rachel; to avoid scandal; to handle the situation with dignity.

**What They Hide**: Her fear about Rachel's behavior; her illness (she dies of heart disease shortly after the theft).

**Key Actions**: Dismisses Sergeant Cuff to protect Rachel; dies in London, leaving Rachel her fortune; her death elevates the family's vulnerability.

---

### Mr. Murthwaite

**Role/Position**: Famous traveler and expert on India; attended the birthday dinner.

**What They Know**: Recognizes the three Indians as Brahmins, not common jugglers — high-caste men on a sacred mission to recover the Diamond.

**What They Want**: To observe; he has scholarly interest in the Diamond and the Indian pursuit.

**What They Hide**: The full significance of what he knows about the Indians' religious devotion.

**Key Actions**: Confronts the Indians in their own language, alarming them; provides crucial context about the Diamond's sacred importance; later traces the Diamond's return to India and witnesses it restored to the Hindu shrine.

---

### Mr. Luker

**Role/Position**: London money-lender; dealer in pledged valuables.

**What They Know**: Receives the Diamond as security for a loan (from Godfrey); holds it in his bank vault for a year.

**What They Want**: Profit; security; to stay out of trouble.

**What They Hide**: The identity of who pledged the Diamond to him.

**Key Actions**: Holds the Diamond; is attacked by the Indians seeking its location; transfers the Diamond back to Godfrey when the loan period ends, enabling the Indians to track and kill Godfrey.

---

### The Three Indians (Brahmins)

**Role/Position**: High-caste Brahmin priests disguised as traveling jugglers; sworn to recover the sacred Moonstone stolen from their temple.

**What They Know**: The Diamond's sacred history; that it was taken from Seringapatam; that it is now in England; they track it through the novel with patient determination.

**What They Want**: To return the Moonstone to its rightful place in the Hindu shrine.

**What They Hide**: Their true caste and sacred mission beneath their disguise as common entertainers.

**Key Actions**: Appear at the Verinder house on Rachel's birthday; track the Diamond from the house to London to Luker; murder Godfrey Ablewhite in a hotel room and recover the Diamond; return it to India where it is restored to the deity's forehead.

---

### Limping Lucy Yolland

**Role/Position**: Daughter of the fisherman at Cobb's Hole; crippled; Rosanna Spearman's only friend.

**What They Know**: Knows Rosanna loved someone hopelessly; holds Rosanna's posthumous letter; knows the location of the hidden tin case.

**What They Want**: To honor Rosanna's memory; to rage against the gentleman (Franklin) who she believes caused Rosanna's death.

**What They Hide**: Rosanna's letter and its secrets — she guards it fiercely until compelled to give it to Franklin.

**Key Actions**: Provides the letter Rosanna left behind; delivers bitter denunciation of Franklin Blake and the gentry class; serves as Rosanna's voice from beyond the grave.

---

### Dr. Candy

**Role/Position**: Local medical practitioner in Frizinghall.

**What They Know**: Knows medicine; had an argument with Franklin Blake at the birthday dinner about medical science. Secretly administered laudanum to Franklin to prove a point.

**What They Want**: Professional respect; to prove Franklin wrong about dismissing doctors and medicine.

**What They Hide**: That he drugged Franklin's drink as a practical joke — he never confesses this directly.

**Key Actions**: Doses Franklin's evening drink with laudanum, causing the unconscious sleepwalking; catches a severe chill that night and develops brain-fever; his delirium, recorded by Ezra Jennings, provides the clue to the entire mystery; survives but with damaged memory, never fully aware of what he revealed.

---

### Colonel John Herncastle

**Role/Position**: Deceased cousin to Lady Julia; disreputable soldier who stole the Moonstone during the Siege of Seringapatam in 1799.

**What They Know**: Knew he killed the three Brahmin guards protecting the Diamond; knew the curse that followed him; knew he was dying and chose to bequeath the Diamond to Rachel.

**What They Want**: Unclear — possibly revenge on a family that shunned him, possibly genuine affection for Rachel, possibly to pass along the curse.

**What They Hide**: His true motives for the bequest (malice? affection? both?).

**Key Actions**: Murdered the Diamond's guardians in 1799; lived under the curse as a social outcast; bequeathed the Moonstone to Rachel with Franklin as courier; dies before the novel begins but sets all events in motion.

| Franklin entered Rachel's room | Rachel (witnessed it) | Everyone else |

## Character Relationships Map

**Love/Devotion**:
- Rosanna → Franklin (unrequited, fatal)
- Franklin ↔ Rachel (mutual, tested, ultimately fulfilled)
- Miss Clack → Godfrey (infatuation, blind)
- Limping Lucy → Rosanna (devoted friendship)

**Service/Loyalty**:
- Betteredge → Verinder family (lifelong)
- Ezra Jennings → Dr. Candy (professional devotion)
- Penelope → Rachel (faithful service)

**Antagonism/Suspicion**:
- Sergeant Cuff vs. Rachel (suspects her)
- Limping Lucy vs. Franklin (blames him for Rosanna's death)
- Indians vs. whoever holds the Diamond

**Deception**:
- Godfrey deceives everyone (false philanthropy)
- Rachel deceives everyone about what she saw (to protect Franklin)
- Rosanna deceives everyone about the nightgown (to protect Franklin)
